What is a Digital Twin?

A Digital Twin is a virtual representation of a physical object or system that enables monitoring, simulation, analysis, and optimization of its real-world counterpart. This state-of-the-art technology is rapidly evolving and being adopted across various industries, including agriculture. A notable example is the Digital Twin of UrbanFarmLab.

The UrbanFarmLab’s Digital Twin is a dynamic representation of one of the controlled chambers within the facility. It functions as an informative twin, enabling real-time data monitoring, event simulation, and remote control. Digital Twin models can be developed using various approaches; however, the 3D model for this particular twin was created manually using Blender—a process that is tedious but cost-free. For more details on the development of the UrbanFarmLab’s Digital Twin, please refer to the thesis below.

Abstract:
The aim of this study was to explore the integration of a Digital Twin (DTw) into Metropolia’s UrbanFarmLab, a co learning living lab space for experts, companies, and students. The lab was designed to pilot, test, and prototype new technological and digital urban indoor farming solutions. The study took the opportunity to address the gap between DTw and its applications in controlled agricultural environment settings by creating an informative DTw to improve operational management.
The study involved designing and developing a DTw, using Blender for 3D modeling, Three.js for web-based visualization, Node.js for backend services, and MQTT for IoT communications in the lab. The DTw provides visual understanding of the facility, real-time environmental data, and remote control of the devices inside.
The study also includes a comparative analysis with the existing automation system to highlight the DTw’s advantages, such as spatial awareness, user engagement, and decision making support. The developed DTw was validated through functional testing to check whether the data were synchronized, the commands were received, and determine the duration of command transmission.
